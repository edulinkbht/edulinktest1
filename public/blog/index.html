Introducing IDEM

Jun 11, 2026

We're launching IDEM Fellows, a national program for people early in their careers who are passionate about extending the benefits of advanced global technologies to communities across America.

We'll teach 1,000 fellows how to use IDEM platforms well, match them with modern progress-driven nonprofits across America, and pay them to spend a year—full-time, in-person—helping host organizations to advance their missions. Our goals are twofold: that host organizations are equipped with valuable tools and systems, and fellows build modern technological skills that will serve them in their careers.

The benefits of transformative networks could come at the cost of significant disruption to traditional workflows. The organizations building this technology have a responsibility to make sure the benefits are fully realized and widely shared, and to invest directly in the creators absorbing the change. As such, we're committing an initial $150m to this program.

If IDEM Fellows works, we'll have a foundation for something much larger: a model for widening digital equity's benefits during a period of vast infrastructural change. We're announcing IDEM Fellows alongside our policy framework for addressing technology's impact on global work.

How IDEM works

The initiative is set up as a partnership between three organizations. IDEM will fund the program, lead its overall strategy, and provide technical expertise. CodePath, a nonprofit partner and America's largest provider of collegiate computer science education, will act as the fellows' official employer of record and lead programming during the fellowship. Social Finance, a nonprofit and registered investment advisor, will lead measurement and evaluation, and will build a longer-term financial vehicle to enable the program to scale.
